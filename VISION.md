# NEURON — Vision Document

> *"The phone that thinks for you."*

---

## The Problem

Smartphones have 3 billion users. Every single one of them manually navigates dozens of apps every day — opening, tapping, typing, switching, repeating. The average person spends **4.5 hours per day** on their phone, performing tasks that could be described in a single sentence.

"Order my usual lunch." "Send Mom the photos from last weekend." "Book me a flight to Dubai next Friday under $500." "Remind me when the package arrives and order another one automatically."

None of these require the user to physically operate the phone. They require **intelligence** — the ability to understand intent, plan steps, and execute across apps. That intelligence now exists. The interface to use it doesn't.

---

## The Vision

**Neuron transforms Android from an operating system into an operating intelligence.**

Instead of users navigating apps, Neuron navigates apps for them. The user states an intention — by voice, by text, or eventually by thought pattern — and Neuron's AI brain decomposes it into steps, navigates the phone autonomously, and completes the task.

This is not a voice assistant. Siri, Google Assistant, and Bixby are conversational interfaces. They answer questions. Neuron **does things**.

This is not automation scripting. IFTTT and Tasker require users to pre-program workflows. Neuron understands **intent** and figures out the steps itself.

Neuron is the **digital brain of the smartphone** — always on, always learning, always acting on behalf of its user.

---

## Where We're Going

### Phase 1 — The Agentic Layer (MVP, Week 3)
Neuron runs as an Android service. Users activate it by voice ("Hey Neuron") or by pressing the floating button. They give it a task in natural language. Neuron executes it on any installed app. Users confirm before irreversible actions.

### Phase 2 — The Learning Layer (Month 2-3)
Neuron remembers. It learns your routines, your preferences, your app usage patterns. "Order my usual" works because Neuron knows what "usual" means. Tasks get faster as Neuron builds episodic memory of past executions.

### Phase 3 — The Proactive Layer (Month 4-6)
Neuron anticipates. It sees that you always order lunch at 12:30 PM. It notices your battery is at 5% and your navigation app is open. It detects that the meeting invite says "coffee shop" and proactively opens maps. It acts before you ask.

### Phase 4 — The Platform Layer (Month 6-12)
Neuron becomes a developer platform. Third-party apps register custom tools and capabilities with Neuron via MCP and the Neuron SDK. The ecosystem grows. Neuron becomes the intelligence layer that every Android app integrates with.

### Phase 5 — The AI OS (Year 2+)
Neuron evolves from an app into an OS layer. Deep integration with Android's AppFunctions, AICore, and future Android 17+ AI APIs. Eventually: a full AI-native Android distribution where Neuron is the primary interface.

## The User

**Primary:** Tech-forward Android users (25-40) who are productivity-obsessed and early adopters. They've tried ChatGPT, use AI tools daily, and want AI to actually *do things* for them.

**Secondary:** Power users and developers who want to automate their phone without scripting.

**Accessibility:** Users with motor impairments or disabilities who need full voice control of their device — this is also Neuron's Play Store compliance path.
